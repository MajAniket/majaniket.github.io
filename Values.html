Core Values

The Values I live by.

  1. Community is King

There is nothing more important to me than the people I surround myself with. They make up who I am, and I am a big part of them. If I can surround myself with great people, I will also become great at one point.

  1. Discipline is Key

Discipline cultivates greatness. There is no magical fix for life, but only through consistency and discipline do things happen.

  1. Three Types of Strength

There are three types of strength that I can see: mental, physical, and spiritual. Mental strength is the fortitude needed to learn and keep building out on projects. Physical strength is for the world, where pushing, pulling, balancing, and flexibility come into play for me to reach goals. They also provide the most visual results. Spiritual strength is the connection with the universe and with my community, where I can provide something to everyone around me. Each plays a part in the other two strengths, and I canâ€™t fully build out one strength without activating the other two.

  1. Everything is a tool

Everything that I have around me brings me something. A lamp brings me light, a chair gives me physical support, and my family gives me mental support. If I can use each to their fullest, then I can bring something to this world around me. I can be a useful tool for everyone once I know how to use my own.

  1. No Zero Sum Games

Status for me is the worst game I've ever played. It means nothing. Status is a means to an end to get something done, not the actual result itself. But the actual title does not matter one bit.
